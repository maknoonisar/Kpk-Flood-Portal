Study Area

The Area of Interest (AOI) is Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (KP) province in Pakistan, known for its diverse topography ranging from mountains to valleys. KP frequently experiences flooding, primarily due to heavy monsoon rains and glacial melt. This study uses the Analytical Hierarchy Process (AHP) and Weighted Linear Combination (WLC) methods to assess flood hazard susceptibility, incorporating various modeling scenarios. The types of floods in KP include riverine floods, flash floods, and glacial lake outburst floods (GLOFs). The slope angle, drainage density, Topographic Position Index (TPI), lithology, and geology, along with NDVI and land use/land cover (LULC), significantly influence flood events.

Geophysical and Environmental Characteristics of KP
Characteristic Details
Slope Angle Varies significantly, with mountainous areas having steep slopes
Drainage Density High, contributing to rapid runoff during heavy rains
Topographic Position Index (TPI) Shows varied elevation, affecting water flow and accumulation
Lithology Composed of diverse rock types, influencing soil permeability
Geology Characterized by tectonic activity, with several fault lines
NDVI Indicates vegetation cover, which affects water absorption
Land Use/Land Cover (LULC) Includes forests, agriculture, and urban areas, influencing runoff patterns

Source: KP Geophysical and Environmental Data Archive, 2023.
