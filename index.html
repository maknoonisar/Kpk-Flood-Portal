About this Project

The project aims to develop a comprehensive and proactive approach to flood management in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (KP) by utilizing various analytical tools and integrating historical flood data to identify flood-susceptible areas. This involves compiling and analyzing data from diverse sources, including dams, reservoirs, geological surveys, and Land Use/Land Cover (LULC) data, to create detailed maps highlighting high-risk zones. Through geospatial analysis and hydrological modeling, the project examines the region's topography, hydrology, and geological composition to understand and predict flood behavior. By studying land use patterns and incorporating these insights, the project seeks to establish effective flood mitigation strategies. The ultimate goal is to make this flood susceptibility data accessible to all stakeholders, including government agencies, local communities, and disaster management organizations, enabling them to adopt a proactive approach to flood preparedness and response.
